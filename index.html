925fm

In an effort to Save our Scene, 925 was conceived as a virtual club intended for the workday.

We'll bring you uninterrupted livestreamed sets, 40 hours a week, and pay the DJs for their time. These folks are currently facing dire prospects as the clubs are closed, festivals are canceled, and the future of clubbing remains uncertain.

How much will it cost?

The recommended price is $10/mo but we will operate on a pay-what-you-can sliding scale. Consider paying what you normally spend going to clubs each month.

How is my money being spent?

We feel deep gratitude for all the DJs that have spent their time raising money for others during the COVID-19 crisis, although we do not think DJs can or should play for free forever.

DJs receive 100% of the profit until we are able to pay them a reasonable rate. We also provide a method to tip DJs.

"Profit" means subscriptions minus real costs of running the platform: we take no pay ourselves until DJs are being paid fairly. This means that when we have 1000 subscribers, we can pay DJs $25/hr.

Eventually, we will need wages ourselves to keep this running. But the majority of your subscription fee will *always* go towards the performers.

What does the schedule look like?

We will launch with a 40 hour per week schedule (9-5pm UTC-5), but eventually hope to run 24/7 when our resources allow, so that our stream works for everyone's workweek.

We also hope to eventually open other "floors" in our club that expand beyond electronic music, as our user base grows.

Who will perform? What will it sound like?

We hope to fill our schedule with DJs both known and unknown. In the meantime, we've created a Spotify playlist to give you an idea of what to expect.

We are currently building our roster. If you're interested in performing, please fill out an application.

But I already pay for Spotify and can get all the DJ sets I want on YouTube!

Spotify pays artists ~$0.003 per play and most of your subscription fee goes to paying major record labels who have entire departments devoted to finding new ways to avoid paying artists fairly.

YouTube pays channels even less per view, and the ad revenue from YouTube videos goes to the channel, not the performer.

And if you're looking for 40 hours of great music to listen to every week, eventually you're going to run out of RA podcasts and Boiler Room sets. :)

What about after the quarantine?

Although the current conditions of our world necessitate new streams of income for DJs & musicians, post-pandemic there will still be a lot of people who want a live stream of music during their work day, and there will still be a lot of DJs with free time during those hours. We hope to be a reliable income stream for DJs until the singularity.

How do I help?

We would love to talk with anyone in the scene — performers, clubs, labels, record stores, promoters, institutions, media outlets. Email us if you are interested and we'll figure out how to work together.

If you run a live-streaming platform, we could use a partnership to keep costs low.

If you are a DJ and would like to play, fill out our DJ application and we'll be in touch.

And lastly — to pay DJs $25/hr, we just need 1000 users. To pay DJs $100/hr, we need 5000 users.

Be one of them, and tell your friends.
